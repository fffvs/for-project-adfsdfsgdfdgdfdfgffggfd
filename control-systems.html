The RBMK Control Systems Manual provides comprehensive operational procedures and technical specifications for the control and protection systems of RBMK-1000 reactors. This manual covers the SUZ (Sistema Upravleniya i Zashchity - Control and Protection System), SKALA computer system, and all associated instrumentation required for safe reactor operation.

Critical Safety Notice: This manual contains operational procedures that must be followed exactly as written. Deviation from prescribed procedures may result in reactor exploding once again, equipment damage, or radiological release. All operators must be certified and current on training before accessing control systems.

1. Control Rod System (SUZ)

1.1 Control Rod Classification

The RBMK reactor employs 211 control rods divided into functional groups:

Manual Control RR (PP) 139 Manual reactivity control 40 Green

Local Automatic LAR (ЛАР) 12 Local power density control 40 Yellow
Local Emergency LAZ (ЛАЗ) 24 Local emergency protection 40 Blue
Shortened Absorbers USP (УСП) 24 Axial flux shaping 40 Purple

1.2 Control Rod Drive Mechanisms

Servo Drive Specifications

  • Motor Type: Three-phase asynchronous, 2.2 kW
  • Gear Reduction: 1:9800 ratio
  • Linear Speed: 0.4 m/s (40 cm/s)
  • Position Accuracy: ±2 cm
  • Clutch System: Electromagnetic with manual release
  • Cooling: Water-cooled bearing assembly at 45-60°C

Rod Construction

Each control rod assembly consists of:

  • Absorber Section: Boron carbide (B₄C) in aluminum alloy tubes
  • Length: 7 meters active absorber
  • Displacer: Graphite follower, 4.5 meters (pre-1986 design)
  • Telescopic Connection: Water-filled aluminum extension
  • Total Travel: 7 meters operational range

2. SKALA Computer System

2.1 System Architecture

SKALA (System of Centralized Control of Aggregate Equipment) is the primary process computer:

Hardware Configuration

  • Main Processors: Two redundant BESM-4M computers
  • Memory: 16K words magnetic core (48-bit words)
  • Storage: Magnetic drum, 256K words
  • Input Channels: 4,096 analog, 2,048 digital
  • Scan Rate: Full system scan every 1-2 seconds
  • Calculation Cycle: Complete core analysis every 7-10 minutes

Primary Functions

  1. Data Acquisition: Continuous monitoring of all sensors
  2. Core Calculations: 3D neutron flux distribution
  3. Reactivity Balance: ORM calculation and tracking
  4. Alarm Processing: Priority-based alarm management
  5. Historical Recording: Parameter trending and logging
  6. Operator Advisories: Recommended control actions

2.2 SKALA Programs and Codes

COMMON SKALA REQUEST CODES: PRIZM - 3D power distribution map SKAZKA - Control rod positions and worth ORM - Operational reactivity margin POTOK - Channel flow rates TEMPER - Temperature distribution DAVLEN - Pressure monitoring TOPLIVO - Fuel burnup status ISTORIA - Historical data retrieval AVARIA - Emergency parameter dump PROGNOZ - Predictive calculations

3. Reactor Protection Systems

3.1 Emergency Protection (AZ) System

The AZ (Avariynya Zashchita - Emergency Protection) system provides multiple levels of reactor shutdown:

Protection Level Designation Activation Rods Inserted Speed
AZ-1 Preventive Protection Power excursion >10% 24 rods Normal (40 cm/s)
AZ-2 Fast Runback Equipment failure 48 rods Normal
AZ-3 Power Reduction Turbine trip 96 rods Normal
AZ-4 Rapid Shutdown Major malfunction 175 rods Normal
AZ-5 Emergency Scram Manual/Auto trip All 211 rods Gravity fall after 2m

3.2 AZ-5 Activation Signals

AZ-5 SCRAM is initiated by ANY of the following conditions:
  • Manual: Operator button (control room or local panels)
  • Power Excursion: Period <3 seconds or power >120% nominal
  • Pressure Loss: Drum separator pressure <5.0 MPa
  • Flow Loss: MCP flow <80% with power >20%
  • Level Upset: Drum separator level ±50mm from setpoint
  • Seismic: Earthquake detection >6 on MSK scale
  • Electrical: Complete loss of power to control systems
  • ECCS Actuation: Emergency core cooling initiation

3.3 Power Reduction Systems

BAZ (Rapid Power Reduction)

  • Reduces power to 60% in 2-3 minutes
  • Activated on single turbine trip
  • Inserts predetermined rod groups
  • Maintains critical geometry

Local Automatic Zone (LAZ)

  • Protects against local power excursions
  • 12 independent zones
  • 2 LAZ rods per zone
  • Activation at 110% local power limit

4. Operating Procedures

4.1 Reactor Startup Procedure

STANDARD COLD STARTUP SEQUENCE
  1. Pre-Startup Checks (T-4 hours)
    • Verify all control rods fully inserted
    • Confirm MCP cooling water flow >1000 m³/h per pump
    • Check drum separator level at -200mm
    • Verify SKALA operational, no critical alarms
    • Confirm ventilation systems running
  2. Neutron Source Check (T-3 hours)
    • Insert startup neutron sources
    • Verify count rate >2 counts/second on all channels
    • Check source range instrumentation
  3. Rod Withdrawal Sequence (T-2 hours)
    • Withdraw USP rods to 3.5m
    • Withdraw peripheral RR rods in groups of 6
    • Monitor period meter, maintain >30 seconds
    • Achieve criticality at 0.001% power
  4. Power Ascension (T-0)
    • 0.001% → 1%: Manual control, 1 decade/hour
    • 1% → 10%: Switch to AR automatic control
    • 10% → 30%: Heat up primary circuit to 260°C
    • 30% → 100%: 1% per minute maximum rate

5. Emergency Operations

5.1 Emergency Operating Procedures (EOPs)

IMMEDIATE OPERATOR ACTIONS - Memory Items

Loss of Coolant Flow

  1. VERIFY flow reduction on multiple indicators
  2. PRESS AZ-5 button immediately if flow <80%
  3. START backup MCPs within 30 seconds
  4. VERIFY ECCS actuation if flow not restored
  5. MONITOR channel outlet temperatures

Power Excursion Event

  1. OBSERVE period meter and power indicators
  2. INSERT manual rods if period <10 seconds
  3. PRESS AZ-5 if period <3 seconds
  4. VERIFY all rods inserting
  5. ANNOUNCE reactor scram on plant PA

7.2 Radial Power Flattening

Control Strategy

  1. Central Depression: Insert center rods to reduce peak
  2. Peripheral Enhancement: Withdraw edge rods
  3. Fresh Fuel Loading: Place in intermediate zones
  4. Burnup Compensation: Shuffle fuel inward over time

7.3 Axial Power Distribution

Offset Control Methods

  • USP Rods: Control top/bottom power ratio
  • Flow Distribution: Adjust channel throttling
  • Partial Length Rods: Shape axial profile
  • Coolant Temperature: Inlet temperature programming

8.2 MCP Start Permissives

Main Circulation Pumps cannot start unless:

  • Reactor power <20% (prevents flow instability)
  • Drum separator level normal (-600 to +100mm)
  • Suction valve fully open
  • Discharge valve closed (opens after start)
  • Seal water pressure >0.2 MPa above system
  • Bearing oil flow confirmed
  • No electrical faults detected

8.3 Turbine Trip Logic

TURBINE TRIP → REACTOR RESPONSE LOGIC: Single Turbine Trip (1 of 2): ├─→ If Power >60%: BAZ (rapid reduction to 60%) ├─→ If Power <60%: No automatic action └─→ Operator reduces power manually Both Turbines Trip: ├─→ If Power >20%: AZ-3 (scram to hot standby) ├─→ If Bypass Available: Maintain 5-10% power └─→ If No Bypass: AZ-5 (full scram) Additional Trips: - Condenser vacuum <500 mm Hg - Generator electrical fault - Turbine overspeed >3300 RPM - Bearing vibration >100 µm
