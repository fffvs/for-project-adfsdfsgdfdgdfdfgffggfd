The RBMK Maintenance and Testing Procedures Manual provides comprehensive guidelines for the preventive and corrective maintenance of RBMK-1000 reactor systems. This manual covers all aspects of maintenance operations, from routine inspections to major component replacements, ensuring safe and reliable reactor operation while minimizing personnel radiation exposure.

⚠️ RADIATION HAZARD WARNING: All maintenance activities must be performed in accordance with ALARA (As Low As Reasonably Achievable) principles. Personnel must be properly trained, equipped with appropriate dosimetry, and follow all radiation protection procedures. Never enter high radiation areas without proper authorization and protective equipment.

1. Preventive Maintenance Program

1.1 Maintenance Philosophy

The RBMK maintenance program is based on a combination of:

  • Time-Based Maintenance (TBM): Scheduled at fixed intervals
  • Condition-Based Maintenance (CBM): Based on equipment condition monitoring
  • Predictive Maintenance (PdM): Using trend analysis and diagnostics
  • Corrective Maintenance: Repairs after failure detection
  • Online Maintenance: Fuel assemblies can be lifted into and out of the reactor, allowing fuel replenishment while the reactor is in operation

2. Control Rod Drive Maintenance

2.1 Drive Mechanism Components

Controlling the RBMK was a very laborious, mentally and physically demanding task requiring the timely adjustment of dozens of parameters every minute, around the clock, constantly wearing out switches such as those used for the control rods

Major Components Requiring Maintenance

  • Servo Motors: 2.2 kW three-phase asynchronous motors
  • Gear Reducers: 1:9800 ratio planetary gearbox
  • Position Indicators: Synchro transmitters and resolvers
  • Clutch Assemblies: Electromagnetic clutches with manual release
  • Cooling System: Water-cooled bearing assemblies
  • Sealing System: Multiple seal stages to prevent leakage

2.2 Annual Control Rod Drive Inspection

📋 CONTROL ROD DRIVE ANNUAL MAINTENANCE PROCEDURE
  1. Pre-Maintenance Requirements
    • Reactor shutdown and cooled to <100°C
    • Control rod fully inserted and locked
    • Drive power isolated and tagged
    • Area radiation survey completed
  2. Drive Motor Inspection
    • Check motor winding resistance (should be 12-15 Ω per phase)
    • Measure insulation resistance (>10 MΩ)
    • Inspect motor bearings for wear
    • Replace motor brushes if worn >50%
    • Verify motor cooling fan operation
  3. Gear Reducer Service
    • Drain and replace gear oil (ISO VG 220)
    • Check gear backlash (<0.5mm)
    • Inspect gear teeth for pitting or wear
    • Replace oil seals if leaking
    • Torque all mounting bolts to specification
  4. Position Indicator Calibration
    • Zero position verification at full insertion
    • Check linearity over full travel range
    • Calibrate at 0, 175, 350, 525, and 700 cm
    • Verify position accuracy ±2 cm
    • Test position transmitter signals
  5. Clutch System Maintenance
    • Test electromagnetic clutch engagement/release
    • Measure clutch air gap (2-3 mm)
    • Check manual release mechanism
    • Verify clutch holding torque >150 Nm
    • Replace clutch plates if worn

2.3 Control Rod Replacement

⚠️ CAUTION: Control rod replacement is a high-radiation task. Typical dose rates near withdrawn control rods can exceed 100 mSv/hr. Use remote handling tools whenever possible.

Replacement Criteria

  • Boron depletion >30% (determined by reactivity worth testing)
  • Mechanical damage to absorber or follower sections
  • Excessive bow or deformation (>10mm over length)
  • Failed position indication
  • One of the most important post-accident changes to the RBMK was the retrofitting of the control rods. A graphite 'displacer' is attached to each end of the length of absorber of each rod. The manual control rods were replaced by rods of an improved design

4. Graphite Stack Monitoring

4.1 Graphite Degradation Mechanisms

The maximum allowed temperature of the graphite is up to 730 °C. The graphite moderator undergoes several degradation processes:

  • Radiolytic Oxidation: Reaction with residual moisture and oxygen
  • Dimensional Changes: Neutron-induced swelling and shrinkage
  • Stored Energy: Wigner energy accumulation in crystal lattice
  • Thermal Stress: Cracking from temperature gradients
  • Mechanical Wear: From fuel channel movement and vibration

4.2 Monitoring Program

Temperature Monitoring

ℹ️ Graphite Stack Temperature Limits
• Normal operation: 450-650°C
• Alarm setpoint: 700°C
• Maximum allowable: 730°C
• Annealing temperature: >250°C (for Wigner release)

5. Primary Circuit Maintenance

5.1 Main Circulation Pump (MCP) Maintenance

The RBMK employs 8 MCPs (6 operating + 2 standby) with the following specifications:

MCP Technical Data

  • Type: Vertical centrifugal, single-stage
  • Flow Rate: 8,000 m³/hr per pump
  • Head: 200 m H₂O
  • Motor Power: 5.6 MW
  • Speed: 1,000 RPM
  • Seal Type: Mechanical, water-cooled

5.2 MCP Seal Maintenance

📋 MCP SEAL REPLACEMENT PROCEDURE
  1. Preparation Phase
    • Reduce reactor power to allow pump isolation
    • Start standby pump and verify flow
    • Close suction and discharge valves
    • Depressurize and drain pump casing
    • Establish seal cooling water flow
  2. Seal Removal
    • Remove seal housing cover bolts
    • Extract seal cartridge assembly
    • Inspect shaft sleeve for scoring (Ra <0.4 μm)
    • Clean seal chamber and inspect for damage
  3. Seal Installation
    • Install new seal cartridge (do not touch seal faces)
    • Verify seal spring compression (25-30mm)
    • Install seal housing with new gasket
    • Torque bolts to 450 Nm in star pattern
  4. Testing and Return to Service
    • Perform static pressure test at 8.5 MPa
    • Check seal leakage (<10 L/hr)
    • Run pump at minimum speed for 2 hours
    • Monitor seal temperatures (<65°C)
    • Gradually increase to normal operating speed

5.3 Steam Separator Drum Maintenance

Internal Components Inspection

  • Cyclone Separators: Check for erosion and blockage
  • Dryer Elements: Inspect chevron vanes for damage
  • Feed Ring: Verify spray nozzle condition
  • Level Instrumentation: Calibrate differential pressure cells
  • Safety Valves: Test and certify set points

7.2 Reactor Protection System Testing

AZ-5 System Monthly Test

  1. Pre-Test Conditions
    • Reactor power <20% or shutdown
    • All control rods operable
    • SKALA system operational
    • Test mode selected on protection panel
  2. Response Time Testing
    • Initiate AZ-5 signal from each actuation source
    • Scram (shut down) rod insertion time cut from 18 to 12 seconds
    • Verify all rods begin movement within 1 second
    • Confirm 90% insertion within 12 seconds
    • Record individual rod insertion times
  3. Logic Testing
    • Test 2-out-of-3 voting logic
    • Verify bypass capabilities
    • Check permissive interlocks
    • Test manual initiation from all locations

8. Refueling Operations

8.1 Online Refueling System

Fuel assemblies at the Ignalina NPP could be changed without discontinuing the operation of the facility. This is another advantage of the reactors of the channel type

Refueling Machine Specifications

  • Type: Overhead crane with sealed transfer chamber
  • Capacity: 2 fuel assemblies per operation
  • Speed: 15-20 channels per day
  • Positioning Accuracy: ±2 mm
  • Chamber Pressure: Matches reactor pressure (6.9 MPa)

8.2 Fuel Handling Procedure

📋 ONLINE REFUELING SEQUENCE
  1. Channel Selection and Preparation
    • Select channel based on burnup criteria (>20 MWd/kgU)
    • Reduce local power using adjacent control rods
    • Cool channel to <200°C outlet temperature
    • Verify channel flow >30 m³/hr
  2. Refueling Machine Positioning
    • Position machine over selected channel
    • Lower and seal transfer chamber
    • Pressurize chamber to reactor pressure
    • Open channel closure plug
  3. Fuel Exchange
    • Engage fuel assembly with grapple
    • Withdraw spent fuel into transfer chamber
    • Rotate magazine to fresh fuel position
    • Insert fresh fuel assembly into channel
    • Verify proper seating and orientation
  4. Channel Restoration
    • Replace channel closure plug
    • Depressurize transfer chamber
    • Release machine seal and move to next channel
    • Restore normal channel flow
    • Return local power to normal using control rods

10. Radiation Protection During Maintenance

10.1 ALARA Program

Dose Reduction Strategies

  • Time: Minimize exposure duration through training and practice
  • Distance: Use remote handling tools and robotics
  • Shielding: Install temporary lead blankets and water shields
  • Source Reduction: Chemical decontamination and flushing

10.2 Typical Maintenance Dose Rates

Reactor Hall (shutdown) 0.1-1 mSv/hr Co-60, Cs-137 TLD, electronic dosimeter

10.3 Contamination Control

⚠️ CONTAMINATION ZONES:
• Zone I (Clean): <0.4 Bq/cm² alpha, <4 Bq/cm² beta
• Zone II (Controlled): <4 Bq/cm² alpha, <40 Bq/cm² beta
• Zone III (High Contamination): >40 Bq/cm² beta
• Always perform contamination survey when exiting zones

Emergency Maintenance Procedures

Control Rod Drive Emergency Repair

🚨 EMERGENCY PROCEDURE - STUCK CONTROL ROD

If a control rod fails to insert during a scram:

  1. Immediate Actions
    • Verify AZ-5 actuation and other rods inserting
    • Attempt manual insertion from local panel
    • If unsuccessful, activate electromagnetic clutch release
    • Monitor local power and temperature
  2. Follow-up Actions
    • Insert adjacent control rods to compensate
    • Reduce reactor power if necessary
    • Initiate troubleshooting of drive mechanism
    • Prepare for rod uncoupling if required
  3. Recovery
    • Once reactor is stable, plan repair strategy
    • May require reactor shutdown if safety margin inadequate
    • Document failure mode for root cause analysis
