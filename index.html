RBMK Unit 3 refers to the third reactor unit in RBMK nuclear power plants, specifically those of the second generation design constructed in the late 1970s and early 1980s conforming to OPB-82 safety standards. These reactors are graphite-moderated, water-cooled channel-type reactors that can produce a gross output of 1,000 MW of electrical power from 3,200 MW of thermal power. The actual electrical output is 925 MW, not 1,000.

Reactor Building Layout

The RBMK Unit 3 reactor building is a massive structure divided into several key areas:

Core Structure

The reactor core is housed in a reinforced concrete vault measuring from what i can find 21.6m × 21.6m × 25.5m. The core itself consists of graphite blocks arranged in a cylindrical formation with a diameter of, again, from what i can find was 14 meters and height of 8 meters. The active core region measures 11.8 meters in diameter by 7 meters in height.

Structural Schemes

The reactor is built with several structural components called "schemes":

  • Scheme S - Five-meter tall steel structure at the bottom, supporting the active zone weight
  • Scheme OR - Two-meter high cylinder forming part of the biological shield
  • Scheme KZh - Shield made from 16mm thick welded steel with reinforcements
  • Schemes L and D - Water-filled hollow metal reservoirs providing structural support and biological protection
  • Scheme E (Elena) - Three-meter tall upper biological shield weighing 2,000 tons
  • Scheme G - Upper assembly components

Support Facilities

  • Control Room - Located below the deaerator room floor
  • Turbine Hall - Houses two 500 MW turbines
  • Machine Hall - Contains refueling equipment
  • Cooling Systems - Pumping stations and heat exchangers
  • Ventilation Stack - For filtered gas discharge

Control Room Overview

The RBMK Unit 3 control room is the nerve center of reactor operations. It features three main operator workstations:

  1. Reactor Control Station - Manages nuclear processes in the reactor core
  2. Systems Support Station - Controls pumps, hydraulic and gas contours
  3. Turbine Control Station - Oversees turbines and electrical generation

Control Room Panels Detailed Breakdown

1. Reactor Core Displays

The control room features two large circular mimic displays:

Control Rod Position Display (Selsyns Panel)

  • Shows position of all 211 control rods
  • Color-coded dials indicate rod function:
    • Red - Automatic control rods (AR)
    • Yellow - Shortened absorber rods (USP)
    • Green - Manual control rods
    • Blue - Emergency protection rods
  • Each dial shows rod insertion depth from 0-7 meters

Core Channel Cartogram

  • Circular display with 1,661 tiles representing each fuel channel
  • Each tile has a channel number and indicator light
  • Lights illuminate when channel parameters exceed normal ranges
  • Connected to SKALA computer for real-time monitoring

Key Monitoring Systems:

  • Temperature sensors - Monitor graphite, concrete, and structural panels
  • Gas moisture detection - 2,000+ detectors grouped in 26 zones check for channel leaks
  • Scheme monitoring - Real-time status of structural components S, OR, KZh, E, and G
  • SKALA codes - Equipment IDs for requesting detailed readings from the computer

3. Main Circulation System Panels

Seven large panels display the reactor's cooling systems:

Drum Separator Display (Mirrored for Both Loops)

  • Shows two drum separators (31m long, 2.8m diameter each)
  • Pressure, temperature, and water level warnings
  • Steam flow paths to turbines
  • Feedwater injection lines (3 normal + 1 bypass)
  • Connection to 4 main circulation pumps per loop

Main Circulation Pump Status Displays

Each pump (8 total, capacity 5,500-12,000 m³/h) has dedicated monitoring:

  • Lubrication system - Oil flow to bearings with cooling
  • Hydrostatic bearing - Water film status for bottom bearing
  • Seal water injection - Prevents radioactive leaks
  • Motor cooling - Temperature management

Control Rod Channel Cooling

  • Closed loop of purified water
  • Gas stripping devices remove hydrogen/oxygen
  • Heat exchangers cooled by intermediate loop
  • Prevents direct contamination of cooling pond

4. Blowdown and Cooldown Systems

Critical for reactor water chemistry and emergency operations:

  • Purification unit - Removes chemical contaminants from coolant
  • Regenerator - Heat recovery system
  • Aftercooler - Temperature reduction
  • Emergency tanks - High-capacity drainage during cooldown

5. Turbine Control Panels

Two massive displays (one per turbine) showing:

Steam Flow Path

  1. High-pressure steam enters through stop/control valves
  2. Passes through 1 high-pressure cylinder
  3. Goes to 4 intermediate separators/reheaters
  4. Feeds 4 low-pressure cylinders
  5. Exhausts to 4 condensers

Condensate Processing

  • 3 condensate pumps move water from condensers
  • Three-stage ejectors remove dissolved gases
  • 5 regenerative heaters warm condensate using turbine steam
  • Purification removes remaining impurities

Turbine Support Systems

  • Lubrication oil circulation and cooling
  • Bearing temperature monitoring
  • Emergency drain systems
  • 30-meter rotor alignment indicators

6. Electrical Generator Panels

Two smaller panels (one per generator) display:

  • Main transformer connections
  • Grid connection status and load
  • Internal power transformers for reactor systems
  • Synchronization indicators
  • Protective relay status

7. Feedwater and Deaerator Systems

Critical for removing corrosive gases from water:

Deaerator Operation

  • Low-pressure steam injection heats condensate near boiling
  • Dissolved oxygen, nitrogen, and CO₂ are driven out
  • Clean feedwater collects in deaerator tanks
  • 5 feedwater pumps return water to drum separators

Clean Condensate Distribution

  • Supplies makeup water to control rod cooling
  • Refills side biological shield (Scheme L)
  • Provides seal water for pumps

SKALA Computer System

The SKALA (or SCALA) computer system is the brain of RBMK operations:

Capabilities

  • Collects data from approximately 4,000 sources
  • Calculates reactivity margin every 10-15 minutes
  • Provides recommendations to operators
  • Displays channel-by-channel power distribution
  • Uses 1960s computer technology housed in separate room

Operator Interface

  • Request keyboard for entering SKALA codes
  • Digital displays showing requested values
  • Teletype for detailed printouts
  • Connection to core map display tiles

Safety Systems

Reactor Protection Systems

Multiple layers of protection ensure safe operation:

  • Emergency Core Cooling System (ECCS)
    • 12 accumulator tanks with nitrogen-pressurized water
    • 3 pairs of electrical pumps for long-term cooling
    • Heat exchangers for decay heat removal
  • Reactor Control and Protection System (RCPS)
    • 211 movable control rods
    • Automatic scram on parameter exceedance
    • Manual emergency shutdown capability
  • Physical Power Density Distribution Control (PPDDCS)
    • Over 100 radial power monitors
    • 12 axial power distribution sensors
    • Maintains power between 10-120% nominal

Containment Features

  • Pressure suppression pools ("bubbler" pools) on levels +3 and +0
  • Steam distribution corridors for accident mitigation
  • Leaktight compartments around pumps (0.45 MPa overpressure capacity)
  • Reactor cavity handles 0.18 MPa overpressure

Fuel Management

Fuel Assembly Specifications

  • Two sub-assemblies per channel, 18 fuel rods each
  • Total uranium mass: 114.7 kg per assembly
  • Fuel burnup: 20 MW·d/kg
  • Total length: 10.025 m (6.862 m active region)
  • Zircaloy cladding with 2% enriched UO₂ pellets

Online Refueling Capability

The RBMK design allows fuel replacement without shutdown:

  • Refueling machine on gantry crane
  • Capacity: 2 assemblies/day normal, 5/day maximum
  • Pressure equalization before channel opening
  • Spent fuel moved to cooling pond
  • Total fuel inventory: 192 tons under stationary conditions

Post-Chernobyl Modifications

Following the 1986 Chernobyl disaster, all RBMK reactors including Unit 3 types underwent significant safety upgrades:

  • Installation of additional absorbers to reduce positive void coefficient
  • 12 second control rod servo motors (insertion time reduced so the graphite doesn't have time to react)
  • ORM (Operational Reactivity Margin) chart recorders added
  • Enhanced computer control systems
  • Improved operator training and procedures
  • Accident localization systems retrofitted
  • Upgraded emergency core cooling systems so chernobyl does not happen again.
